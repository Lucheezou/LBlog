UI Design

Designing the UI.

By Luke Vincent Samson

Guided by the seasoned design team, I delved into the heart of UI design. The intricacies of crafting interfaces that seamlessly meld user expectations with brand identity became an enchanting puzzle to solve. The foundation was set, and I eagerly rolled up my sleeves to explore further. At Andale, I discovered that UI design was not just about aesthetics, but a delicate balance between aesthetics and functionality. The principles of visual hierarchy, typography, color psychology, and layout design were illuminated in ways I had never imagined. Each element had a purpose, a role to play in shaping user interactions. Hands-on experience took me from static wireframes to interactive prototypes. With tools like Figma, I brought my design concepts to life, mapping out user flows and interactions. This dynamic process allowed me to see my ideas take shape and evolve based on feedback from mentors and colleagues. UI design at Andale was grounded in empathy. Understanding user needs, behaviors, and pain points was a cornerstone of our work. Collaborating with the UX research team, I realized that design decisions were not arbitrary; they were rooted in insights gained from real users.

Author

Luke Vincent Samson

A Computer Science student at Bicol University, currently taking an internship at Andale.
